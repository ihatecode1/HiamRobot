What is ASMR?

ASMR is known as Autonomous Sensory Meridian Response. It is a somewhat pseudoscientific term to describe the physical sensation experienced when triggered by certain sounds or even visuals. This video by HeatherFeather explains it pretty well. U/mjmurcott delves into the science behind ASMR in this explanatory video.

It is strange for those new to it and certainly for those that don't experience ASMR. Unfortunately, it seems that not everyone gets ASMR.

What does ASMR feel like?

It is a tingling sensation that starts in the scalp or on the back of your neck and can run down your body through your extremities. Did anyone ever try to give you the chills like this scene in the office? If it worked for you, then that's exactly what it feels like. It is euphoric and calming.
What is ASMR used for?
For some, it is a novel entertainment experience, but for others it is used for relaxation or to induce sleep. It can be used to treat depression, anxiety, panic attacks, and headaches.
What types of ASMR exist?
ASMR is separated into two categories. Intentional (where the ASMRtist is trying to induce ASMR) or Unintentional (like listening to a professional softly describe his work). The subcategories are role-plays and trigger videos. Role-plays try to entertain and produce tingles. Role-plays could be anything, but some of the popular role-plays are a doctor appointment, an eye exam, a cranial nerve exam, or getting a haircut. Trigger videos vary a lot, but some common triggers are tapping,whispering, soft-speaking, crinkling, scratching, ear cupping, ear cleaning, mic brushing, kinetic sand, mic squishing, foam sounds, anticipatory ASMR, sk/kiss sounds, inaudible whispering, soft singing, layered sounds, and mouth-sounds. Mouth sounds? Yeah, it seems strange and I don't like hearing them in real life, but they do work for me very well through headphones.

Visual Triggers
These can be languid hand movements like tracing, having hands run slowly down your "face," or even just precise hand movements.
Can you get ASMR in real life?
Absolutely. I think most people who get ASMR have experienced it in real life. I can get it if someone has a very nice voice and is softly speaking or whispering. The real life trigger that gets me the most is when I'm laying on my bed and one of my dogs comes up and sniffs the back of my head. That's actually the strongest and most consistent trigger I've experienced.
Is ASMR sexual?
Inherently no. There are a few ASMRtist that make it somewhat sexual, but it is by no means the norm. People can make anything sexual and ASMR is no exception. It is there if you are specifically looking for it, but the large majority of videos are not sexual.

Where to start?
Well, it would probably be best to go through a trigger assortment video to identify what triggers work for you. This video by HeatherFeather is the most comprehensive. If you can identify some triggers, then you can find lots of videos that will work for you with that trigger.
Just a reminder: ASMRtists are people too. They have thoughts, emotions, and a life outside of ASMR. Please be respectful of their feelings and their time. Please do not leave hateful comments, creepy comments, or pushy "when is the next video, you have been taking for ever, wtf?" comments. Also, please do not DOX them (or anyone for that matter).

Other Common Questions
ASMR Immunity?
You can develop an immunity if you listen to too much ASMR. You build a tolerance for it and you get triggered less often and they can be less intense. The recommended solution is to take a break for a while. For some people it is two weeks, for others a month; it isn't a one solution fits all situation. Just take a break, try it again, if it doesn't work, take another longer break and so on until you get ASMR again. Springbok ASMR gives her thoughts on it there.
Why do some sounds make me irrationally angry?
You likely have a (minor, mostly harmless) disorder called Misophonia. Most ASMRtist have videos dedicated to a certain trigger, so you should be able to avoid whatever sound it is that you hate.
Does anyone else only get ASMR in just one ear?
Yup this is fairly common. I have not heard any theories as to why this happens.
I only get ASMR from unintentional videos. Is that weird?
Not at all. There is a whole subreddit dedicated to just unintentional videos. See r/unintentionalASMR.
Is it unusual to only get whispered ASMR from foreign language speakers?
No. I think it has a correlation to inaudible whispering in that you can't anticipate the word and your mind doesn't try and decipher it. In any case, there are quite a few people like you, which is why very many ASMRtists have videos in other languages. Here is a 35 video playlist that someone made, but that barely scratches the surface of what is out there. I knowFast ASMR has a few videos in her native tongue (German), SoftlyGaloshes has many videos in Spanish, FairyChar has videos in "Fairy Language" (I'm not sure exactly which language that is), GentleWhispering has a Russia teacher role-play (she's from Russia), Miss ASMR and The French Whisperer have videos in French, and I'm sure that you could find a video in all the major languages. I even found one in Afrikaans by ASMR Cleaner.
I don't get ASMR :( Can I develop it?
It is not certain if it is possible or not, but it does not seem to be. Some ASMRtists will say that they believe there is a trigger for everyone and that you just have to find it, but there is no conclusive evidence.
Do people who don't get ASMR still watch the videos? And if yes, why?
There are a good number of ASMRless watchers out there. They watch the videos, because they find ASMR to be soothing and relaxing.
